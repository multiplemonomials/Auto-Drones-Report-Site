Drone Regulations

With the recent advent of autonomous drones as a product for the masses, governments have been struggling to create rules for how they can and can't be used.

Why Regulate?

In general, people view government regulations as wet blankets that limit people's freedom to do what they want. However, there have been several times, both intentional and accidental, when a drone caused a scare or put people in (some) danger. So, regulation of drones may in fact be warranted. For example, in December 2014, a drone flying near Heathrow Airport nearly crashed into a landing plane. It didn't cause damage, but if it had hit the plane, it could have been bad (Brown 2014). In another case, a drone carrying radioactive sand was landed on top of the Diet Building in Tokyo.
